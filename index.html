Context

1960s-1970s Japan As Interface Language

Transit Precision

Navigation and rhythm follow rail signage logic: concise labels, high contrast, and direct pathways through each section.

Print-Era Contrast

Display serif and geometric sans mirror magazine covers and product catalogs from the high-growth Showa period.

Architectural Framing

Blocks, borders, and measured spacing draw from concrete facades, exhibition posters, and modular interiors.

UX Notes

Built For Clarity And Flow

  • Hierarchy First

    Every section leads with one clear headline and one direct action.

  • Motion With Purpose

    Staggered reveals and ticker movement establish pace without noise.

  • Mobile Respect

    Grid collapses intentionally, preserving rhythm and typography on smaller screens.
