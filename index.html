    What The Masters Teach

    Design Principles Distilled Into Interface Rules

    01 Kamekura: Silence Carries Force

    Give focal shapes breathing room and let emptiness do structural work instead of crowding every surface.

    02 Tanaka: Tradition Can Be Systemic

    Historic visual language becomes sharper when translated through repeatable modules and disciplined spacing.

    03 Yokoo: Saturation Needs Narrative

    Density, collage, and contradiction only work when the interface still gives the eye a clear story to follow.

    04 Nagai + Fukuda: Symbols Must Think

    Visual wit is not garnish. Forms should compress meaning, not merely decorate the page.

    05 Ishioka + Sugiura: Editorial Space Is Emotional

    Typography, color, and spatial framing should feel authored, persuasive, and unmistakably intentional.
